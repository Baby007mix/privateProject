Artistic Talent

VOICES FROM CHERNOBYL

Today, there is a story to be told. A story of the victims: the wives, workers, politicians, scientists, ordinary people who lived through it and continue to suffer its consequences. Thanks to the works of Svetlana Alexievich we have access to their memories.

Oleg Sidorchik

Oleg graduated from the Belarusian State Academy of Arts and performed at many theatres in Belarus before joining the Belarus Free Theatre (BFT), a world-renowned group. When the BFT was banned Oleg fled to London and has since been performing in numerous productions in London, incluing in the Globe Theatre and The Young Vic.

Julia trained at RADA on scholarship. TV includes Love Again (BBC), Mankind(BBC), Letters from Chechnya(BBC). Theatre includes Rossetti's Woman, (Yvonne Arnauld), Mistresses (Lichfield Garrick), Humble Boy(New Vic), Underground (Barbican and Brighton Theatre Royal) and Beatrice in Much Ado About Nothing (directed by Mark Rylance for LTI)

Karina Knapinska

Karina trained at ARLA and graduated from ActorWorks in 2012. She performed in several plays, short films and variety of productions to date. Her theatre credits include Dying for it (Jack Studio), Lost Angel (RoterHaare), No Rhyme (Jack Studio), Dinner (Riverside), Joy (The London Theatre) and Better Humans (Battersea Arts Centre).

Kim Christie

Kim started training in Hoxton Hall Community Theatre under the wonderful Margaret Sheppard. She trained at ActorWorks and at The Poor School. Kim formed part of the 5-star production of Hang at Edinburgh Fringe in 2016. Other Roles include Andromache from Women of Troy, Polina Andryevna from The Seagul and Billy Downs in The Libertine.

Germán D’Jesús

Germán trained in Italy with performer M. Giovinazzi and ActorWorks and over the past few years has adapted and directed a number of Political performances including Voices from Chernobyl (London), Bilal (Rome), Love and Terror in the Wind (London, Italy & Venezuela) and Lost Angel in a Hostile City (London and Vienna).

Lev Levermore

Lev made his UK debut at the Almedia Theatre in BOY directed by Sacha Wares. His recent theatre credits include Richard III directed by Rupert Goold at the Almedia Theatre staring Ralph Fiennes and Vanessa Redgrave. Before Lev moved to England he worked with a repertory theatre in Moscow.

Natalia Bogdanova
